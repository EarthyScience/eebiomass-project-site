            The second <b>EEBIOMASS Summer School</b> took place from <b>21–25 October 2024</b> at the <b>Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry</b> in Jena. We received a total of <b>102 applications</b> (33% female, 28% from German institutions), representing a 50% increase compared to the first EEBIOMASS Summer School in 2022.

            A total of 43 applicants were invited to attend the summer school in person in Jena. Eight participants declined the invitation for various reasons. In the end, <b>35 participants</b> attended the summer school in Jena (17 women, 18 participants from German institutions, <b>21 nationalities</b>).

            The summer school focused on the different aspects of the ESA Earth Explorer mission <b>BIOMASS</b>, introducing participants to the mission objectives, technical background (including <b>SAR, tomography, interferometry, and polarimetry</b>), and related research topics (such as <b>above-ground biomass</b> and the carbon cycle).

            We were pleased to welcome two external speakers: <b>Björn Rommen</b> from the European Space Agency presented the BIOMASS mission, and <b>Anja Rammig</b> from the Technical University of Munich shared her research on forest dynamics in the Amazon region. In addition to lectures and hands-on exercises, participants had the opportunity to present their own research through talks or poster sessions and discuss their work with experts on site.

            📌 <b>Teaching materials</b>, including lecture slides, Jupyter notebooks, and exercise guidelines, were made available to participants online.
